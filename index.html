OTHER COUNTRIES

  • FRANCE

    People who work overtime are considered to be those who cannot produce results within the allotted time, and vacations are taken company-wide. Many restaurants offer "Happy Hour", a service that allows people to eat and drink at a discount starting from dinner time, and it is a common sight for businessmen to have a drink after work. The law allows for five weeks of paid vacation, so you can take a month off in the summer.

  • SPAIN

    There is a style of working until past noon, taking a siesta break for about three hours, and then working again in the cooler evening hours.

  • US

    Another characteristic of the U.S. is that there is no legal obligation to grant paid work. Secondly, the American employment system is characterized by job-based employment, discretionary labor, and performance-based employment. In Japan, full-time employees are members of a company and can be transferred to any department or location within the company, but in the U.S., white-collar employees are generally hired for a specific job, position, or location.

HOW TO WORK IN CHINA

Concentrate and work hard within a set time frame. Do not like to work overtime. 　Tend to work only what their salary will cover. 　Work hard at the task at hand. Do not plan ahead. 　Do not manage risk or crisis. Think about how to deal with problems only after they occur. 　Do not pay much attention to their superiors. Always express your true feelings. 　Often makes decisions and acts on his or her own without reporting to or consulting with superiors. 　If they disagree with their supervisor or colleagues, they discuss it thoroughly. Proactively change jobs in search of better conditions. 　If a good idea comes to mind, they will implement it immediately. They like challenges. 　Results-oriented.
